Artificial intelligence is transforming how we process and understand audio content. Speech recognition systems have evolved significantly over the past decade, moving from simple command recognition to full conversational transcription. Modern multimodal models like MIMO V2.5 can understand not just the words being spoken, but also the context and meaning behind them. This enables more accurate transcription even in challenging acoustic environments. The system we are building today demonstrates this capability by providing a user-friendly interface for audio transcription that can handle files up to one hour in length. This is achieved through intelligent audio chunking with overlapping segments that ensure no words are lost at chunk boundaries. Each chunk is independently transcribed and then merged back together using a sophisticated overlap detection algorithm. Chapter 2. In addition to basic transcription, our pipeline integrates with several advanced AI agent tools. The circular dependencies agent can clean up and restructure the raw transcript into a more coherent document. The atomic graph agent can extract key concepts and their relationships from the transcript, building a knowledge graph that reveals the underlying structure of the ideas discussed. Finally, the RAG document assistant serves as a second brain, storing transcripts for future retrieval and semantic search. This creates a powerful ecosystem where audio content is not just transcribed, but truly understood and made accessible for future reference. The integration of these tools transforms a simple transcription task into a comprehensive knowledge management workflow.
